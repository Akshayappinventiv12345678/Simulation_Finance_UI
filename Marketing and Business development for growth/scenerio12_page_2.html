Financial Simulation
Scenario 12: Capturing Seasonal Sales Demand

Concepts

Assumptions:

In a manufacturing organization, assets—particularly inventory and accounts receivable—are closely tied to sales. When sales increase due to seasonal demand, the company will need to produce more goods, which leads to an increase in inventory. Additionally, the increase in sales will result in higher accounts receivable as customers purchase more products.

Impact on Cash Flow: Cash flow from operating activities is influenced by changes in sales, COGS, and collections from customers. The reduction in sales means less cash inflow, while the decrease in COGS represents less cash outflow for production.

Cash Flow Components:

  • Cash Inflow (from Sales): The increase in sales of INR 60 Lakhs will eventually convert into cash, during the next period. Cash Collected = 0% of INR 60 Lakhs = INR 0 Lakhs.
  • Cash Outflow (COGS): The cost to produce the additional sales will be reflected in cash outflows for raw materials, labor, and other direct costs. Increase in COGS = 50% of INR 60 Lakhs = INR 30 Lakhs.

Net Impact on Cash Flow:

  • Net Increase in Operating Cash Flow = Cash Collected from Sales – Cash Outflow for COGS.
  • Net Operating Cash Flow = INR 0 Lakhs (collected from sales) – INR 30 Lakhs (spent on COGS).
  • Net Change in Operating Cash Flow = -INR 30 Lakhs.
