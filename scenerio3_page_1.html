Scenario 3: Participating in RFQ for New Bids

Market Condition:A leading manufacturing company in the region has issued a Request for Quotation (RFQ) for a large order of industrial components. The MSME sees this as a chance to increase revenue by securing new business from a reputable client.

Options for Management:

1. Submit a Highly Competitive RFQ:The company can submit a quotation with attractive pricing and terms to win the contract.

  • Pros: Quick business growth and expansion
  • Cons: Competitive pricing could lead to thinner margins

2. Target Niche or Custom Products for the RFQ:Instead of competing on price, the company could focus on offering custom or value-added services in its RFQ.

  • Pros: Differentiation may reduce competition.
  • Cons: Requires additional customization resources.

Investment Required:

  • INR 1,00,000 costs associated with preparing the RFQ (Request for Quotation).
  • INR 2,00,000 customization costs for offering value-added services in the RFQ.

Output Gained:

  • INR 30,00,000 Revenue from winning the RFQ.
  • INR 25,00,000 Revenue from customized product RFQ.
