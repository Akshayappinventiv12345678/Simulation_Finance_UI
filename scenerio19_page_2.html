Financial Simulation
Scenario 19: Merger and Acquisition - Acquire Smaller Competitor

Assumptions:

The full acquisition increases asset and inventory values on the balance sheet but depletes cash reserves. If goodwill is involved, it reflects the excess paid for the acquisition. The alternate option (partnership) involves a smaller cash outlay, reducing risk but potentially limiting control over the new business.

Financial Statement Implications

P&L Statement:

  • Acquisition: The acquisition itself does not immediately affect the P&L. However, any future expenses related to integration or operational costs may impact profitability.
  • Strategic Partnership: The investment in the joint venture (INR 10 Lakhs) will be recorded as an expense if it incurs operational costs or losses in the joint venture.

Balance Sheet:

  • Acquisition: The inventory (INR 20 Lakhs) and property, plant & equipment (INR 30 Lakhs) will be recorded as assets.
  • Strategic Partnership: The investment in the joint venture (INR 10 Lakhs) will be recorded as an asset under "Investment in JV."

Cash Flow Statement:

  • Acquisition: The cash outflow of INR 50 Lakhs for the acquisition will be recorded under investing activities.
  • Strategic Partnership: The cash outflow of INR 10 Lakhs for the investment in the joint venture will also be recorded under investing activities.

Cash Flow Statement (Specific Entries):

  • Acquire smaller competition:
    • Debit: Investing Activities (INR 55,00,000)
    • Credit: Cash and Cash Equivalents (INR 55,00,000)
  • Strategic Partnership:
    • Debit: Investing Activities (INR 10,00,000)
    • Credit: Cash and Cash Equivalents (INR 10,00,000)

Equity Method Investments:

Equity method investments are recorded as assets on the balance sheet at their initial cost and adjusted each reporting period by the investor through the income statement and/or other comprehensive income (OCI) in the equity section of the balance sheet. The investor should measure the initial value for an equity method investment in the common stock of an investee at cost.
