Scenario 3: Implementing Additive Manufacturing (3D Printing) - Results

Discussion

Assumptions:

  • Investment in Advanced Manufacturing:
    • The company records capital expenditures by:
      • Debiting the Property, Plant & Equipment account for the purchase and installation of 3D printing equipment and automated inspection systems.
      • Crediting the Cash/Bank account to reflect the outflow of funds.
    • Expenditures on staff training programs are recorded as Administrative Expenses, highlighting the operational costs associated with implementing new technologies.
    • Output Gained from Investment:
      • Increased Accounts Receivable and Sales Revenue, indicating higher sales from faster market entry and enhanced production capabilities.
      • Cost savings from reduced material waste and improved quality are captured by debiting the Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) account, thereby lowering overall production costs.
    • At the end of the accounting period, Closing Entries are made to transfer the balances of Sales Revenue and COGS to Retained Earnings, ensuring that the financial statements accurately reflect the company’s profitability and maintain the balance sheet’s integrity.
  • Outsourcing Prototype Development:
    • The company records expenses related to:
      • Partnering fees
      • Contract management
      • Quality assurance audits
      by debiting Administrative Expenses and crediting Cash/Bank.
    • This approach allows GlobalFusion to leverage external expertise without significant capital investment.
    • Output Gained from Outsourcing:
      • Cost savings on capital investment.
      • Increased revenue from access to specialized expertise, reflected by debiting COGS and crediting Sales Revenue.
      • Operational efficiencies and faster development times contribute to further revenue increases and expense reductions.
    • Similar to the first option, Closing Entries are performed to transfer the balances of Sales Revenue and adjusted COGS to Retained Earnings, ensuring that the financial records remain balanced and accurately represent the company’s financial performance.
  • Investments in long-term assets (like manufacturing equipment) are capitalized, meaning they are recorded as assets and depreciated over time.
  • Operational costs (such as staff training and maintenance) are expensed immediately, affecting the income statement directly.
  • Understanding how strategic investments translate into financial performance is crucial. Investments lead to increased revenue and cost savings, which are reflected in Sales Revenue and reduced COGS, respectively.
