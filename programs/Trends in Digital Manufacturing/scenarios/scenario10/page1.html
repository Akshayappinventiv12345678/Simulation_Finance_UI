Financial Simulation
Scenario 2: Sudden Decrease in Sales due to Market Competition

Market Condition:

  • Situation: A new competitor enters the market offering similar products at lower prices. This results in a sudden dip in sales for the MSME by 10%.
  • Why it Occurred: The competitor has introduced a cost-cutting strategy and is able to offer products at lower prices, which affects the MSME’s pricing power and market share.

Options for Management:

  • 1. Short-term: Adjust the Marketing Budget
    • Pros: Quick response, can regain market share without heavy investments.
    • Cons: May not solve the long-term pricing competition.
  • 2. Medium-term: Introduce a New Product Line
    • Pros: Differentiates the product offering and opens new revenue streams.
    • Cons: Requires R&D investment and time to market.

Short-term Solution: Adjust Marketing Budget

  • Investment Required: INR 5 Lakhs for promotional campaigns and customer outreach.
  • Output Gained: A 5% increase in sales, leading to an additional INR 20 Lakhs in revenue.

Medium-term Solution: Introduce a New Product Line

  • Investment Required: INR 20 Lakhs for R&D, prototyping, and initial production of the new product.
  • Output Gained: An estimated additional INR 50 Lakhs in sales revenue from the new product line. COGS for the new product line is 60% of the sales revenue.
