Decision Making and Problem Solving

Program Overview

The Decision Making and Problem-Solving Program is designed to equip mid-career professionals and leaders with essential decision-making models and problem-solving methodologies. Participants will learn techniques for product development, process improvement, risk management, and mitigation strategies.

Through realistic simulation scenarios, participants will enhance their ability to analyze complex situations, develop effective solutions, and mitigate risks within the dynamic manufacturing environment of the fictional organization GlobalFusion. These simulations reflect real-world complexities in a competitive manufacturing landscape.

Mini Case Study: Navigating Bidding Processes and Market Challenges

Market Situation:

GlobalFusion Industries, a leading supplier in the motor vehicle aftermarket industry, has experienced steady growth driven by increasing demand for sustainable manufacturing solutions. Operating in a highly competitive environment, strategic decision-making and problem-solving are crucial for maintaining and expanding its market presence. As new opportunities and challenges arise, GlobalFusion must effectively navigate bidding processes and address internal and external disruptions.

When a new competitor introduces a disruptive product, leading to a significant drop in GlobalFusion’s sales and market share, the company seeks to strengthen its relationship with key customers and showcase new product lines effectively through a focused in-person event.

Through these scenarios, learners at GlobalFusion will tackle the intricacies of bidding for projects, responding to competitive pressures, and organizing customer-centric events. By applying decision-making models and problem-solving methodologies such as SWOT analysis and root cause analysis, participants will develop the skills necessary to navigate complex market dynamics and sustain GlobalFusion’s competitive advantage.

Key Highlights

  • Comprehensive Training: Focuses on decision-making models like SWOT analysis and cost-benefit analysis.
  • Practical Methodologies: Includes problem-solving techniques such as root cause analysis and brainstorming.
  • Risk Management: Teaches strategies to identify and mitigate potential risks.
  • Real-World Simulations: Engages participants with challenges that mirror actual industry scenarios.
  • Strategic Advantage: Equips leaders to maintain and enhance their organization’s market position.
