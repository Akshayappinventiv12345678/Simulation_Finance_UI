Scenario 2: Participating in Tenders concepts

Concepts

Techniques for responding effectively to increased competition, focusing on differentiation, customer engagement, strategic marketing and calculating key performance indicators (KPIs) such as Return on Investment (ROI), Economic Value Added (EVA), and Operating Cash Flow (OCF) to make informed decisions about tender participation.

1. Return on Investment (ROI)

Measures the profitability of an investment relative to its cost, indicating the efficiency and effectiveness of the investment.

  • Ensure all expenditures related to the investment (i.e., interest on loans) are accounted for to avoid skewed ROI.
  • Establish minimum acceptable ROI levels (e.g., 13% ROI guidance from finance team) to guide investment decisions.

Formula:

ROI = (Net Profit / Investment Cost) × 100%

Where:

  • Net Profit: Revenue - Cost

Example:

Use KPIs to compare the financial viability of government versus private tenders.

  • Government Tender:
    • Investment Cost: INR 2,00,000
    • Potential Revenue: INR 60,00,000
    • Net Profit: Potential Revenue minus all associated costs.
    • ROI Example: If Net Profit is INR 10,00,000, then ROI = (10,00,000 / 2,00,000) × 100% = 500%

2. Economic Value Added (EVA)

Assesses a company's financial performance by calculating the value created beyond the required return of its shareholders.

Formula:

EVA = Net Operating Profit After Taxes (NOPAT) − (Capital Invested × Cost of Capital)

Helps determine if a project/tender will add value after covering the cost of capital. A positive EVA indicates value creation. Prioritize projects/tenders with higher ROI and positive EVA, ensuring profitable investments.

Formula:

Operating Cash Flow (OCF): OCF = Net Income + Non-Cash Expenses − Increase in Working Capital

Ensures the company maintains healthy cash flow while undertaking the tender. Positive OCF indicates sufficient liquidity. Monitor OCF to maintain operational stability during the bidding process.

Example:

  • If NOPAT is INR 8,00,000, Capital Invested is INR 10,00,000, and Cost of Capital is 10%:
  • EVA = 8,00,000 − (10,00,000 × 10%) = 8,00,000 − 1,00,000 = INR 7,00,000
  • If Net Income is INR 10,00,000 and Non-Cash Expenses are INR 2,00,000:
  • OCF = 10,00,000 + 2,00,000 − Increase in Working Capital

3. Competitive Response Strategies

Techniques for responding effectively to increased competition by focusing on differentiation, customer engagement, and strategic marketing.

  • Differentiation: Offering unique products or services that set the company apart.
  • Highlight compliance expertise for government tenders.
  • Emphasize innovative solutions and cost-effectiveness in proposals.
  • Customer Engagement: Building strong relationships with clients through communication and responsiveness.
  • Engage with government agencies or private clients to understand their specific needs.
  • Provide tailored solutions that address client pain points.
  • Strategic Marketing: Promoting the company's strengths and capabilities effectively.
  • Enhance brand visibility through targeted marketing campaigns.
  • Showcase successful past projects and client testimonials.
