Marketing and Business Development for Growth
Scenario 1: Reactivating Dormant Sales Channels

Concepts

To effectively reactivate dormant sales channels and expand market reach, executives at GlobalFusion Industries should be well-versed in the following four theoretical concepts from the Marketing and Business Development for Growth Program.

Sales Channel Re-activation

Techniques for revitalizing inactive sales channels to expand market reach and increase revenue.

1. Market Segmentation and Targeting

Market Segmentation involves dividing a broad market into distinct subsets of consumers with common needs or characteristics. Targeting then focuses on selecting the most viable segments to pursue with tailored strategies.

  • Utilize CRM data to categorize dormant channels by factors such as past sales volume, region, and product specialization.
  • Focus reactivation efforts on segments with higher growth rates and larger market shares to maximize return.
  • Develop tailored re-engagement strategies for each segment based on their unique characteristics and needs.

Key Formulas:

  • Segment Size = Number of Potential Customers in Segment
  • Growth Rate = (Current Year Segment − Previous Year Segment) / Previous Year Segment × 100%
  • Market Share = (Company’s Sales in Segment / Total Sales in Segment) × 100%

Application:

  • Identify Viable Segments: Analyze dormant distributors and resellers based on their past performance, geographic location, and potential for growth.
  • Evaluate Segment Potential: Use segment size and growth rate to prioritize which dormant channels to target first.

2. Marketing Mix Optimization (4 Ps)

The Marketing Mix comprises four key elements: Product, Price, Place, and Promotion. Optimizing each element ensures that the company's offerings meet customer needs while achieving business objectives.

  • Ensure that the products offered through reactivated channels meet current market demands and client specifications.
  • Use price competitiveness index to ensure that pricing strategies are attractive to distributors yet sustainable from a company profitability perspective.
  • Reactivate and potentially expand the number of distribution channels to maximize market reach in targeted regions.
  • Implement targeted promotions, such as exclusive discounts or limited-time offers, to encourage immediate re-engagement to incentivize dormant channels to re-engage.

Key Formulas:

  • Product Fit Score = Number of Features Aligned with Customer Needs / Total Number of Features × 100%
  • Price Competitiveness Index = (Company’s Price / Average Market Price) × 100%
  • Distribution Coverage = (Number of Active Channels / Total Potential Channels) × 100%
  • Promotion ROI = (Revenue Generated from Promotion / Promotion Costs) × 100%

Application:

  • Ensure product alignment with market needs to enhance sales channel effectiveness.
  • Use competitive pricing strategies to attract and retain distributors.
  • Expand distribution networks strategically to cover high-potential markets.
  • Implement promotions that drive immediate sales and re-engagement.

3. Branding and Positioning Strategies

Branding involves creating a strong, positive perception of the company and its products in the minds of customers. Positioning refers to how the company's products are perceived relative to competitors, emphasizing unique value propositions.

  • Strengthen the brand image to be seen as reliable and innovative, increasing the attractiveness of re-engaging channels.
  • Ensure all reactivation campaigns and communications consistently reflect the company’s brand values and messaging.
  • Clearly communicate unique benefits and value-added services to distinguish GlobalFusion from competitors in RFP responses such as superior product quality, customization capabilities, or exceptional customer support.
  • Use testimonials and case studies from existing customers to build credibility and trust with dormant channels.

Key Formulas:

  • Brand Equity = Brand Awareness × Brand Loyalty × Perceived Quality
  • Positioning Index = (Perceived Unique Value / Competitive Value) × 100%
  • CSAT = (Number of Satisfied Customers / Total Number of Responses) × 100%

Application:

  • Strengthen brand attributes to enhance channel attractiveness.
  • Maintain consistent branding across all reactivation efforts.
  • Differentiate products through unique value propositions to stand out in the market.
  • Leverage customer feedback to build trust and credibility.
