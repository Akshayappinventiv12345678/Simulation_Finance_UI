Scenario 1: Reactivating Dormant Sales Channels

Market Condition: The company has previously established sales channels, including distributors, resellers, and agents, who have become inactive over the past few years. These dormant channels represent an untapped source of potential revenue. By re-activating these channels, the company can leverage existing relationships and expand sales without significantly increasing operational costs.

Options for Management:

1. Launch a Channel Re-activation Program: Offer special incentives and exclusive deals to dormant distributors and resellers to encourage them to re-engage with the company’s product line.

  • Pros: Leverages existing relationships with minimal additional costs.
  • Cons: Success depends on the willingness of dormant channels to re-engage.

2. Offer Profit-Sharing Incentives: Instead of traditional commissions, the company could offer profit-sharing arrangements to distributors and resellers, making it more attractive for them to resume selling the company’s products.

  • Pros: Aligns incentives with channel partners, encouraging long-term commitment.
  • Cons: Reduces margins in the short term.

Investment Required:

  • INR 1,00,000 expenses for running the channel reactivation campaign and 5% of sales as Commission Expense for Channel Reactivation offering incentives to inactive distributors.
  • INR 2,00,000 expenses for profit-sharing arrangements with distributors and resellers and 5% of sales as Commission Expense for Profit-Sharing Incentives.

Output Gained:

  • INR 20,00,000 Revenue from re-activated sales channels.
  • INR 25,00,000 Revenue from sales through profit-sharing incentives with resellers.

Or, instead of across-the-board cost-cutting, the company could focus on streamlining specific operations using lean management techniques. This could include eliminating waste, improving inventory management, or automating repetitive tasks to reduce costs sustainably.
