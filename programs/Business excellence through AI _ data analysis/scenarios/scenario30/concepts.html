1. Cost Benefit Analysis

Cost Benefit Analysis involves comparing the total expected costs of an initiative against the total expected benefits to determine its financial viability and strategic value.

  • Include all direct and indirect costs such as maintenance, training, and downtime during installation.
  • Estimate tangible benefits like increased production efficiency, reduced defect rates, and higher sales.
  • Assess how changes in key assumptions (e.g., cost savings, revenue growth) affect the CBA outcomes.

Key Formulas:

  • Cost-Benefit Ratio = Total Benefits / Total Costs
  • Net Benefit = Total Benefits − Total Costs

Executives use cost-benefit analysis to prioritize projects with the highest return on cost. Options with a ratio significantly greater than 1 are preferred.

Use the ratio to assess which strategy offers the highest return (tangible benefits like increased sales, market share, and long-term revenue growth) relative to its cost. Ensure that all direct and indirect costs (such as R&D, marketing, and operational expenses) are accounted for to avoid skewed results.

Examples:

  • Fully Adopt Cutting-Edge Manufacturing Technologies with AI Integration
    • Total Costs: INR 62 Lakhs
    • Total Benefits: INR 78 Lakhs
    • Cost-Benefit Ratio: (78,00,000 / 62,00,000) = 1.26 (or 126%)
    • Net Benefit: 78,00,000 − 62,00,000 = 16,00,000 INR
  • Gradual Enhancement of Existing Systems with Selective Technology Upgrades
    • Total Costs: INR 47 Lakhs
    • Total Benefits: INR 54 Lakhs
    • Cost-Benefit Ratio: 54,00,000 / 47,00,000 = 1.15 (or 115%)
    • Net Benefit: 54,00,000 − 47,00,000 = 7,00,000 INR

2. Return on Investment (ROI)

Measures the profitability of an investment relative to its cost, indicating the efficiency and effectiveness of the investment.

  • Ensure all expenditures related to the investment (i.e., interest on loans) are accounted for to avoid skewed ROI.
  • Establish minimum acceptable ROI levels (e.g., 13% ROI guidance from finance team) to guide investment decisions.

Formula:

ROI = (Net Profit / Investment Cost) × 100%

Where:

  • Net Profit = Revenue - Cost

Use KPIs to compare the financial viability:

  • Fully Adopt Cutting-Edge Manufacturing Technologies with AI Integration
    • Investment Cost: INR 62 Lakhs
    • Net Profit: INR 16 Lakhs
    • ROI: (16,00,000 / 62,00,000) × 100% ≈ 25.81%
  • Option 2: Gradual Enhancement of Existing Systems with Selective Technology Upgrades
    • Investment Cost: INR 47 Lakhs
    • Net Profit: INR 7 Lakhs
    • ROI: (7,00,000 / 47,00,000) × 100% ≈ 14.89%

3. Predictive Modeling

Predictive Modeling involves developing algorithms that analyze historical and real-time data to forecast equipment maintenance needs. These models help in predicting failures before they occur, allowing for timely interventions.

  • Train machine learning models using collected IoT data to predict equipment failures.
  • Incorporate various data types and sources to enhance model accuracy.
  • Continuously refine machine learning models with new data to maintain high prediction accuracy.

Key Formulas:

  • Prediction Accuracy (%) = (Correct Predictions / Total Predictions) × 100%
  • Forecast Error (%) = ​(Actual Value − Predicted Value) / Actual Value ​× 100%
  • False Positive Rate (%) = (Incorrectly Predicted Failures / Total Predictions) × 100%

Optimize models to reduce unnecessary maintenance actions. Perform regular validation against real-world outcomes to ensure reliability.

  • EMV for Technological Obsolescence = 0.3 × 20,00,000 = 6,00,000 INR
