Scenario 2: Optimize Pricing Strategy

Discussion

Organization incurs administrative expenses, which are recorded as costs spent to pursue a new business opportunity. The organization anticipates receiving payment from the customer for supplying the components, recognizing this amount as sales revenue, which reflects the income from the new contract. To fulfill the large order, the organization purchases additional inventory, ensuring they have enough materials to produce the required components. As production begins, the costs associated with making the products are transferred from inventory to the cost of goods sold, representing the direct expenses tied to manufacturing. Finally, when the customer makes the payment in the next quarter, the organization records the cash received, reducing the amount owed by the customer and increasing its cash reserves.

Assumptions:

In most businesses, COGS varies directly with sales, meaning that when sales increase or decrease, COGS will typically increase or decrease proportionally. In this scenario, let's assume that COGS is 50% of sales.

Impact on Cash Flow:

Cash flow from operating activities is influenced by changes in sales, COGS, and collections from customers. The reduction in sales means less cash inflow, while the decrease in COGS represents less cash outflow for production.

Cash Flow Components:

  • Cash Inflow (from Sales): When sales decrease by INR 40 Lakhs, the company will receive INR 40 Lakhs less from customer payments over time. This directly reduces cash inflows.
  • Cash Outflow (COGS): With a reduction in production, the company will also spend INR 20 Lakhs less on raw materials, labor, and other direct costs, reducing cash outflows.

Net Impact on Cash Flow:

  • Net Decrease in Cash Flow = Decrease in Cash Inflow (Sales) - Decrease in Cash Outflow (COGS).
  • Net Cash Flow Impact = INR 40 Lakhs (Sales Decrease) - INR 20 Lakhs (COGS Decrease).
  • Net Decrease in Cash Flow = INR 20 Lakhs.

Thus, the overall cash flow from operating activities will decrease by INR 20 Lakhs due to the 10% reduction in sales, even though the company saves INR 20 Lakhs on COGS.
