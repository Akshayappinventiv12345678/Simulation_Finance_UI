Scenario 3: Cash Flow Crisis Due to Delays in Customer Payments

Market Condition: The company is experiencing a negative cash flow crisis due to delays in customer payments beyond the agreed credit terms, leading to liquidity issues. Current outstanding receivables have been delayed beyond their expected period by an average of 30 days. The company needs to act quickly to address the issue of limited cash to cover its operational expenses including payroll and supplier payments. The MSME is already stretched, with high accounts receivable but low cash reserves.

Options for Management:

  • 1. Tighten credit terms (shortening the payment period or requiring upfront payments)
    • Pros: Quicker realization of receivables into cash, improving liquidity.
    • Cons: May strain customer relationships or reduce sales.
  • 2. Increase penalties for delayed payments:
    • Pros: Shortens the time to cash realization, improving cash flow.
    • Cons: May strain customer relationships or reduce sales.

Investment Required: Minimal monetary investment but involves operational adjustments. This may strain customer relationships or reduce sales.

Output Gained:

    1. Tighten Credit Terms:

  • Improved cash flow as receivables are collected faster.
  • Potential loss of some customers due to stricter terms.
  • Reduction in outstanding receivables by 20%.

    2. Increase Penalties for Delayed Payments:

  • Encourages timely payments, improving cash flow.
  • May lead to reduced customer satisfaction and potential loss of business.
  • Reduction in outstanding receivables by 15%.
