Financial Simulation
Scenario 4: Bid for a New Contract

Assumptions:

  • The company plans to invest INR 10 Lakhs to hire new sales personnel and set up a new office to support its bid for a large industrial contract.
  • This investment is considered a financing activity because it is a long-term, strategic investment aimed at increasing the company's future revenue through expanding its market presence and capabilities.

Breakdown of the INR 10 Lakhs Investment:

  • Hiring Costs: Includes salaries, recruitment fees, and onboarding expenses for new sales staff.
  • Office Setup Costs: Expenses related to leasing office space, purchasing office equipment, and setting up infrastructure to support the sales team's operations.

The total investment is INR 10 Lakhs, which is spent immediately, reducing the company's cash reserves. The investment in the sales team is not just an operational expense but a strategic long-term investment aimed at securing a large contract. The company expects this investment to result in a steady revenue stream in the future if the contract is won, making it a financing activity rather than a purely operating one.

  • SG&A Expenses: Since this is a long-term investment, it is classified under SG&A rather than COGS. Expanding the sales team and setting up an office are considered part of the company's administrative costs to increase its capacity.
  • Impact on Cash Flow Statement: The INR 10 Lakhs outflow will be reflected as a negative financing cash flow because it represents cash spent on long-term growth initiatives.

For “Product Innovation” option:

If the organization invests INR 10 Lakhs for R&D expenses and INR 15 Lakhs for PP&E expenses, it will have long term benefits for the company than just a boost in one season from marketing expenses. It is a short-term vs long term decision, higher risk for higher rewards.
