Scenario 1: Reaching Out to Old Inactive Customers

Market Condition: The company identifies a list of previous customers who have been inactive for over a year. These customers could be re-engaged through targeted sales visits, phone calls, and special offers. This approach could reignite relationships and result in new orders from previously inactive clients.

Options for Management:

  • 1. Re-engagement Campaign:
    • Pros: Relatively low-cost way to increase sales.
    • Cons: Response rates may be unpredictable.
  • 2. Personalized Sales Visits:
    • Pros: Builds stronger relationships and higher chances of re-engagement.
    • Cons: Higher time and resource investment.

Investment Required:

  • INR 50,000 costs for marketing, phone calls, and sales visit efforts for re-engaging customers.
  • INR 1,50,000 investment for personalized sales visits to top inactive customers.

Output Gained:

  • INR 10,00,000 Revenue from re-engaged customers through calls and campaigns.
  • INR 20,00,000 Revenue from sales visits and personalized deals.
