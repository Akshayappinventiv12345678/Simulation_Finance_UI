Concepts

To effectively re-engage inactive customers and revive sales, executives at GlobalFusion Industries should be well-versed in Forecasting and Scenario Planning, Cost-Benefit Analysis, and Return on Investment (ROI), from the Finance for Growth Program. These concepts are practical, easy to comprehend, and can be directly applied to day-to-day corporate decision-making.

Resource allocation and cost estimation, Customer Relationship Management (CRM), and Reactivation Strategies – Techniques for re-engaging inactive customers to revive sales, enhance customer lifetime value, and strengthen customer relationships.

1. Forecasting and Scenario Planning

Forecasting involves creating a detailed financial plan outlining expected revenues and expenditures for specific projects. Monitoring entails tracking actual performance against the budget to ensure financial objectives are met.

  • Break down costs into categories (e.g., marketing, personnel) for better tracking.
  • Conduct monthly / quarterly budget reviews to identify variances and take corrective actions.
  • Be flexible to reallocate funds based on project performance and changing circumstances.

Trend Analysis:

  • Examine past sales data to forecast potential revenue growth from re-engaged customers.

Scenario Planning:

  • Develop different financial scenarios (best-case, worst-case) to prepare for uncertainties (e.g., changes in variables like costs or revenues impacting overall profitability).

Key Formulas:

  • Variance = Actual Cost − Budgeted Cost
  • Budget Utilization Rate = (Actual Spending / Budgeted Amount) × 100%

Direct more resources to high-impact projects while ensuring that lower-priority initiatives are adequately supported. Ensure that short-term projects are funded to meet current opportunities. Allocate resources to long-term initiatives that enhance operational capabilities and competitive advantage.

Application:

  • Re-engagement Campaign
    • Budget Utilization Rate = (Actual Spending / Budgeted Amount) × 100%
    • Re-engagement Campaign budget has a planned expenditure INR 50,000. Track marketing, phone calls, and sales visit costs to ensure they stay within budget.
  • Personalized Sales Visits
    • Budget Utilization Rate = (Actual Spending / Budgeted Amount) × 100%
    • Personalized Sales Visits Budget planned expenditure is INR 1,50,000. Monitor costs related to customized solutions and deals during sales visits.

2. Cost Benefit Analysis

Cost Benefit Analysis involves comparing the total expected costs of an initiative against the total expected benefits to determine its financial viability.

  • Include all direct and indirect costs such as maintenance, training, and downtime during installation.
  • Estimate tangible benefits like increased production efficiency, reduced defect rates, and higher sales.
  • Assess how changes in key assumptions (e.g., cost savings, revenue growth) affect the CBA outcomes.

Key Formulas:

  • Cost-Benefit Ratio = Total Benefits / Total Costs
  • CBA Ratio > 1 ⇒ Beneficial Investment
  • Net Benefit = Total Benefits − Total Costs

Executives use cost-benefit analysis to prioritize projects with the highest return on cost. Options with a ratio significantly greater than 1 are preferred.

Application:

  • Re-engagement Campaign:
    • Costs: INR 50,000
    • Benefits: INR 10,00,000 revenue
    • Ratio: 10,00,000 / 50,000 = 200 (Highly favorable)
  • Personalized Sales Visits:
    • Costs: INR 1,50,000
    • Benefits: INR 20,00,000 revenue
    • Ratio: 20,00,000 / 1,50,000 ≈ 133.33 (Favorable)

3. Return on Investment (ROI)

ROI measures the profitability of an investment relative to its cost, indicating the efficiency and effectiveness of the investment.

  • ROI = (Net Profit / Investment Cost) × 100%
  • Where Net Profit = Revenue - Cost
  • Use KPIs to compare the financial viability.

Application:

  • Re-engagement Campaign:
    • Net Profit: INR 10,00,000 - INR 50,000 = INR 9,50,000
    • ROI: (9,50,000 / 50,000) × 100% = 1900%
  • Personalized Sales Visits:
    • Net Profit: INR 20,00,000 - INR 1,50,000 = INR 18,50,000
    • ROI: (18,50,000 / 1,50,000) × 100% ≈ 1233.33%
