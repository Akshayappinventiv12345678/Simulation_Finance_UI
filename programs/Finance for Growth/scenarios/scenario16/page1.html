Scenario 5: Cash Flow Crisis - Pursue External Financing

Market Condition: Due to delays in customer payments, the company is unable to maintain positive cash flow. External financing is needed to cover short-term obligations of INR 30 Lac for at least a month. The company can pursue financing options like short-term debt or lines of credit from financial institutions like Banks or NBFCs.

Options for Management:

  • 1. Obtain a Short-term Debt of INR 30 Lac from the Bank:
    • Pros: Immediate inflow of cash to cover operating expenses.
    • Cons: Increases the company’s debt burden and interest expenses.
  • 2. Use a Line of Credit of INR 30 Lac:
    • Pros: Flexible access to funds as needed.
    • Cons: May have higher interest rates and fees if not managed properly.
  • 3. Extend Payment Terms with Suppliers:
    • Pros: Provides temporary relief without increasing debt.
    • Cons: May strain relationships with suppliers and could lead to supply chain issues.

Investment Required:

  • Short-term Debt: INR 30 Lac with interest costs and potential fees.
  • Line of Credit: INR 30 Lac with flexible drawdown and interest based on usage.

Output Gained:

  • Cash: INR 30 Lac received as one-month financing from the Bank or accessible via the line of credit.
  • Interest Expense: Increased due to financing costs and short-term repayment obligations.
  • Extended Payment Terms: Temporary relief from immediate cash outflows without increasing debt.
