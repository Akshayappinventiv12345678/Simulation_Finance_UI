Scenario 3: Balancing Design for Manufacturability (DFM) with Aesthetic Design - Results

Outcome of Your Decision

The company has chosen to prioritize aesthetic design with enhanced DFM adjustments. This involves investing in advanced design tools, prototyping equipment, and workforce training to ensure that products are both visually appealing and manufacturable at scale.

Concepts:

Strategic Investment: Balancing investments in advanced design tools with optimizing existing resources to maximize ROI and maintain operational stability.

Operational Efficiency: Leveraging DFM principles to enhance manufacturability without compromising on essential aesthetic features.

Risk Management: Proactively identifying and mitigating risks associated with design adjustments and market competition to ensure smooth implementation.

Stakeholder Engagement: Involving key stakeholders in the evaluation process to gather diverse insights and secure buy-in for chosen strategic initiatives.

Investments in CAD/CAM software, simulation tools, and prototyping equipment are capitalized, while training, consulting, and administrative expenses are expensed.

Financial Impact:

  • Capital expenditures on advanced software and equipment.
  • Increased operating expenses due to training, consulting, and maintenance.
  • Revenue increase from enhanced product aesthetics and market competitiveness.
  • Cost savings from optimized manufacturability and reduced design iterations.

Assumptions:

The investments will lead to a 10% increase in sales revenue. Operating expenses have increased due to training and consulting costs. COGS has decreased due to cost savings from optimized manufacturability.
